The Game

A one-day seminar wargame

Participants manage sustained U.S.–PRC strategic competition using non-military instruments. The Green Team plays the partners and allies whose choices decide whether the U.S. alliance structure holds or fractures.
Blue

United States — sets economic statecraft.

Green

Red

Why we play

Purpose

Explore how alliance cohesion is built or broken when a great power leans on economic statecraft, and surface the inflection points where partner action reinforces or undermines the U.S.-led order.
GREEN · Fractured Order3 / 31
Your mandate

Green Team objective

  • Protect national interest first.Security, economy, and domestic politics of your own state set your bottom line.
  • Decide your alignment.How far you move with Blue, how far you hedge toward Red, and on what terms.
  • Manage exposure.Trade, supply-chain, and technology dependence shape what you can actually afford to do.
  • Keep your options open.Autonomy is a resource; do not spend it cheaply for either side.

Every move

The three standing questions

Question 1

How are you positioned?

Your security, economic, and political starting point this move.

Question 2

What changes with Blue?

How U.S. action shifts your calculus — for better or worse.

Question 3

What changes with Red?

How PRC / Russia pressure or incentive moves you.

Your answers are your move. Be concrete: name the action, the actor, and the condition.

Per move

Green Team tasks

  • Assess your exposure.Where Blue or Red action actually reaches your economy and security.
  • State your conditions.What you need from Blue to move, or from Red to stay.
